Section 03

Set Up Google Business Profile

This is how you show up on Google Maps and Google Search. Free. Takes 10 minutes to set up, 5-7 days to verify.

1
Go to business.google.com
Sign in with your new business Gmail (not your personal one). Click "Manage now" or "Add your business."
2
Enter your business name
Type your exact business name (e.g., "Wickham Lawn Care"). If Google suggests an existing business, click "Add your business to Google" to create a new one.
3
Pick your category
Start typing your business type. Pick the closest match:
Junk Removal: "Junk dealer" or "Waste management service"
Lawn Care: "Lawn care service"
Pressure Washing: "Pressure washing service"
House Cleaning: "House cleaning service"
Car Detailing: "Car detailing service"
Pool Cleaning: "Swimming pool cleaning service"
Event Decor: "Event planner" or "Party supply rental service"
Pet Sitting: "Pet sitting service"
Photography: "Photographer"
Social Media: "Marketing agency"
4
Set your service area
Choose "I deliver goods and services to my customers" (you go to them). Set your area to your city + surrounding towns (about 25 miles). Do not add a physical address unless you have a storefront.
5
Add your phone number and website
Phone: Your BOSS business number (the one Boston gave you — NOT your personal number).
Website: Leave blank for now. Boston will set up your page.
6
Set your hours
Your AI answers 24/7, but set your listed hours to when you actually do jobs. Example: Monday-Saturday, 7am-7pm. The AI will still take calls outside these hours and schedule for the next available day.
7
Write your description
Keep it short and local. Here's a template:
[Business Name] provides [service type] in [City] and surrounding areas. Locally owned and operated. We answer every call, provide upfront pricing, and show up when we say we will. Call or text for a free estimate.
8
Upload 3-5 photos
Google profiles with photos get 42% more direction requests. Upload:
1. A cover photo (your truck, equipment, or you working)
2. A before/after photo of a job
3. You or your team (builds trust)
Don't have job photos yet? Take photos of your equipment/vehicle for now. Replace with job photos after your first gig.
9
Verify your business
Google will send a postcard to your home address with a verification code. Takes 5-7 days. When it arrives, enter the code at business.google.com. Your profile won't show on Google Maps until verified. Some businesses can verify by phone or email — Google will offer it if available.
Do not skip this

Google Business Profile is how customers find you when they search "[your service] near me." Without it, you're invisible on Google. It's free. Do it today.

Section 04

Create Your Facebook Business Page

Facebook is where most of your first customers will come from. Marketplace posts + a business page = calls within 48 hours.

1
Go to facebook.com/pages/create
Log into your personal Facebook account (or create one if you don't have it). Click "Create Page."
Page name: Your business name exactly as it appears on Google Business
Category: Search for your business type (same categories as Google)
Bio: One sentence. Example: "Junk removal in McKinney TX. Same-day available. Call for a free quote."
2
Add your profile picture and cover photo
Profile picture: A logo, your truck/equipment, or you in work mode. Square format.
Cover photo: A before/after shot, your vehicle, or a job in progress. Wide format (820x312).
3
Fill out the About section
Phone: Your BOSS business phone number
Email: Your business Gmail
Hours: Same as Google Business Profile
Location: Your city and state (service area, not home address)
Website: Leave blank for now
4
Set up your CTA button
On your page, click "Add Action Button" → select "Call Now" → enter your BOSS business phone number. This puts a call button right on your page.
5
Join local community groups
Search Facebook for "[Your City] community" or "[Your City] marketplace." Join 3-5 groups. Do not spam them. Wait until Section 06 for posting strategy.
Optional but recommended

Instagram: Same name as your Facebook page. Link them so posts go to both. Especially important for event decoration, photography, detailing, and cleaning (before/after photos perform well).

Nextdoor: Create a business account at business.nextdoor.com. Nextdoor is gold for local services — homeowners actively look for recommendations there.

Section 07

Running a Job Start to Finish

Every job follows this exact same flow.

1
AI books the job. You get notified.
When a customer calls your number, the AI gives them a price, picks a time, and books the appointment. You receive a notification with all the details. Confirm the job by showing up.
2
Show up on time. Introduce yourself.
"Hey, I'm [name] from [business name]. I'm here for the [service]. Let me take a look." Walk the job with the customer, confirm what they need, and confirm the price before you start.
3
Do the work. Do it well.
Take a "before" photo. Do the job. Take an "after" photo. These photos become your marketing material — post them (with customer's permission).
4
Collect payment.
The AI sends a payment link after the job. If the customer wants to pay cash or Venmo directly, that works too. Collect before you leave. Don't "send an invoice later."
5
Log the job.
Go to bosssystems.co/log-job.html and log the job details: customer name, service, amount collected. This keeps your records straight for Sunday reporting.
6
The AI handles the rest.
After you log the job, the system automatically sends a payment confirmation and a Google review request to the customer. You don't need to do anything else.

Section 09

The Non-Negotiables

Break these and we end the partnership. No exceptions.

  • Never flake on a booked job. If a customer is booked, you show up. Period. If you have a genuine emergency, text Boston AND the customer immediately.
  • Report every Sunday. Even if it's $0. Skipping a report without explanation = 1 warning. Skip twice = partnership ended.
  • Report accurately. The AI logs bookings as a cross-check. If your reported numbers consistently don't match what the AI logged, we have a problem.
  • Don't copy, resell, or reverse-engineer the AI system. The tech is BOSS Systems' IP. Your business is yours. The tech is not.
  • Be professional. You represent your own business. Don't show up late, don't show up messy, don't argue with customers. Handle complaints calmly.
  • Safety first. If a job feels unsafe, walk away. No job is worth an injury. Especially for junk removal: wear gloves, closed-toe shoes, and watch for sharp objects. For lawn care: hydrate constantly in summer heat.

Section 12

Taxes and Legal

This is not legal advice. Talk to a parent or tax professional. But here's what you should know.

1
You're an independent contractor, not an employee
BOSS Systems doesn't employ you. You run your own business. That means no taxes are withheld from your earnings. You are responsible for your own taxes.
2
Track every expense
Gas, dump fees, supplies, equipment, phone bill — anything you spend on the business. Keep receipts or screenshots. These reduce your taxable income. Use a spreadsheet or a notes app — just keep track.
3
If you earn over $600, you may receive a 1099
If BOSS Systems pays you more than $600 in a year (through the 80/20 split), you may receive a 1099-NEC form. This is normal for independent contractors. You report this income on your tax return.
4
Talk to a parent about insurance
Some businesses benefit from liability insurance (especially junk removal, pressure washing, and lawn care). Your family's homeowner's insurance might already cover some small business activity. Ask a parent before your first job.
Keep it simple

Most summer partners earn under $10K — which means a simple tax return. The key is tracking your expenses now so you're not scrambling in January. Take a photo of every receipt.
