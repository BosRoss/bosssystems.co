What scenario does the mystery shopper use?
The AI calls your business as a homeowner with a pest problem. It might describe finding droppings in the kitchen, seeing termite swarmers on the porch, or dealing with ants taking over the bathroom. It asks about availability, pricing, and what to expect the same way a real customer would. Your staff won't know it's a test.

That's exactly why this matters. If you're crawling under a house and can't answer, your customers experience the same thing. 85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back. They call the next company. Better to find out for $29 than lose a $1,200 annual contract to a competitor who picks up.

Within 1 hour of payment. The call happens within minutes. Grading and report generation take a few more minutes. Your full report lands in your inbox typically within 30 minutes.
Will my office staff know it's a mystery shop?
No. The AI sounds like a real homeowner describing a real pest problem and asking real questions about scheduling and treatment. Your team won't know they're being evaluated, so you get an honest picture of what your customers actually experience when they call.

That's the most important data you can get. If your business phone goes to voicemail during business hours, you'll get an F for Answer Speed and a clear picture of how many calls you're likely losing. 62% of service calls go unanswered. Now you'll know if you're one of them.
Should I test during bug season or the slow months?
Both tell you different things. Peak season (spring and summer) shows how you handle the rush when termite swarmers, ants, and mosquitoes drive call volume through the roof. Off-season shows your baseline. Many owners do a monthly shop to track improvement and catch problems before they cost real money.

Yes. That's one of the best uses for Phone Grade. We call the number your customers call. We grade whoever answers, whether it's your front desk, an answering service, or voicemail. Many pest control owners discover their answering service is giving wrong information about treatment types or failing to book the inspection.
