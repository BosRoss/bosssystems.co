### THE SETUP (say this BEFORE they call)

**Step 1: Plant the seed (15 seconds)**

> "Before I explain anything, I want you to experience it. I have a demo line set up — it's going to answer like it's YOUR receptionist. I want you to call it and pretend you're a customer whose [AC just broke / pipe is leaking / roof is damaged] and you need someone today. Just talk to it like you'd talk to a real person."

**Step 2: Hand them the scenario**

For HVAC:
> "Pretend it's 9 PM, your AC quit working, it's 95 degrees in your house, and you need someone tonight."

For Plumber:
> "Pretend your kitchen is flooding from a burst pipe under the sink and you need someone NOW."

For Roofer:
> "Pretend a big storm just came through, your ceiling is leaking, and you need someone to look at it tomorrow morning."

For Junk Removal:
> "Pretend you just cleaned out your garage and you've got a couch, a mattress, and 10 bags of stuff that need to go this weekend."

**Step 3: Give them the number**

> "Here's the number — just call it right now."

Then SHUT UP. Let them call. Do not explain anything while they're on the phone. Let the AI do the work.

### DURING THE CALL

**What Boston does:** Nothing. Stand there. Look calm. Let the prospect experience the product without commentary.

**What the prospect should experience:**
1. Immediate pickup (no rings = no voicemail)
2. Natural greeting with the business name
3. Empathy about their situation ("that's rough, especially with kids" / "I'm sorry about that")
4. Smart questions (address, urgency, what exactly happened)
5. Pricing transparency (range, not "I'll have someone call you")
6. Immediate booking with specific time options
7. Confirmation with name read back
8. Professional close in under 2 minutes

**The "wow" moment:** It should come around the 15-second mark when the AI asks a smart follow-up question instead of just saying "someone will call you back." The prospect will look at Boston with wide eyes. That is when you know you have them.

**Ideal demo call length: 60-90 seconds.** Long enough to be impressed, short enough to stay interesting. The agent should wrap up naturally — not drag.

---

### AFTER THE CALL — THE CLOSE SEQUENCE

**Step 1: The Observation (5 seconds after they hang up)**

> "Notice how it got your address, figured out the urgency, and booked you an appointment — all in about a minute?"

**Step 2: The Implication (the money line)**

> "That just happened at 9 PM on a Thursday. Your phone is off. You're with your family. And a $1,500 job just got booked instead of going to voicemail."

**Step 3: The Math**

> "How many calls a week do you think go to voicemail when you're on a job or after hours?"

Let them answer. Whatever they say, use it:

> "Even if it's 3 calls a week — at your average job value, that's [their number x their average job] walking out the door every month. This costs $50."

**Step 4: The Comparison**

> "Ruby Receptionist charges $250 a month for a real person. Smith.ai charges $95 plus overages. This does the same thing for $50 flat — no overage bills, no surprises."

**Step 5: The Risk Removal**

> "Try it for 30 days. If it doesn't pay for itself in the first month — which it will from one single after-hours call — cancel. No contract. Venmo me $50 and it's live by Friday."

---

### COMMON OBJECTIONS (and what to say)

**"It sounds like a robot"**
> "Did it sound like a robot when you called just now? You talked to it for 90 seconds — did you feel like you were talking to a machine, or someone who worked there?"

**"My customers want to talk to a real person"**
> "They want to talk to someone who can help them. Right now they're getting voicemail and calling the next number. This answers every time. Which do your customers prefer — an AI that books them, or a voicemail they don't leave?"

**"I'm too small / not enough calls"**
> "That's exactly who this is perfect for. You can't afford a $250/month answering service. You can afford $50. And every call it catches is money you were already losing."

**"I need to think about it"**
> "What specifically? If it's the price — it's $50, less than a single job. If it's whether it works — you just heard it work. What part needs more thinking?"

**"I already have someone who answers my phone"**
> "What happens at 9 PM? What happens on Saturday? What happens when they're on another call? This covers the gaps — it doesn't replace your person, it backs them up."

## THE DEMO SEQUENCE THAT MAXIMIZES CLOSE RATE

1. Walk in, brief intro (30 sec)
2. "Call this number, pretend you're a customer" (they call, 60-90 sec)
3. Debrief: point out what it did (30 sec)
4. "Now call YOUR own number" (they call themselves, hit voicemail, 15 sec)
5. The contrast hits. They see the gap.
6. Math: "X missed calls x your average job = $X/month you're losing"
7. Price: "$50/month. Try it 30 days. Cancel anytime."
8. Close: "I can have it live by Friday. Venmo $50 to get started."

Total time: 5-7 minutes from walk-in to close attempt.

## KEY PRINCIPLES

- The demo IS the pitch. Don't explain the product — let them experience it.
- The contrast between the demo and their own voicemail is more powerful than any stat.
- 60-90 seconds is the sweet spot. Longer = boring. Shorter = not impressive enough.
- HVAC is always the strongest demo in summer (May-August). Lead with it.
- Never apologize for being 17. Confidence in the product is all that matters.
- If they say "that's pretty cool" — you're 80% there. Close immediately, don't oversell.
- After-hours is the killer feature. Frame everything around "what happens at 9 PM."
