## DECOY/ANCHOR PITCH (for BUILD or higher)

**In-person order (say it this way):**
1. Name the target first: "Most contractors go with BOSS BUILD — $1,497 setup, $97/month. Full AI system: phone, CRM, follow-ups, scheduling."
2. Anchor up: "The full transformation is $5,997 — we rebuild your whole operation. Some of our larger clients go that route."
3. Offer the entry: "And if you just want the phone answered — $250 setup, $50/month."

**Why this works:** First thing named = strongest impression. $5,997 makes $1,497 feel reasonable. $50 feels like nothing. The middle always wins.

**Revenue share kills objections:** "Or zero upfront and 15% of what the AI catches." → They do the math and realize $97/mo is way cheaper. Drives them to fixed price.

## VOSS QUICK REFERENCE (use these live)

**Accusation audit (walk-in):**
"I know what you're thinking — here's some kid trying to sell you something. You probably think AI can't handle real calls. That's fair."
→ Then silence. They'll ask what it is.

**Labeling (when they share pain):**
"It seems like answering the phone isn't the problem — it's the calls that come in when you're on a job."

**Mirror (get them talking):**
Repeat their last 1-3 words as a question. "Miss some calls?" → they elaborate.

**Calibrated questions (price pushback):**
"What would need to happen for you to feel comfortable trying this?"

**No-oriented questions:**
"Would it be ridiculous to try this for a month at $50?"

## WALK-IN SCRIPT (60 seconds)

**Second 0-3:** Walk in. Eye contact. Nod. Wait for them to speak first.

**Second 3-5:** They say "What can I do for you?"

**Second 5-10:** "Hey, are you the owner? I'm Boston — I live here in Tyler. Got something I want to show you real quick, about 60 seconds."

**Second 12-20:** "I actually called your shop yesterday around [time] as a customer. [What happened: rang out, voicemail, nobody picked up.] If I'm a customer whose pipe just burst, I'm calling the next guy."

**Second 20-25:** PAUSE. Let the pain land. Say nothing.

**Second 25-30:** "I know you're out on jobs all day — every contractor I talk to has the same problem."

**Second 30-35:** Pull out phone. "Call this number and hear what your customers would hear instead."

**Second 35-55:** They call demo. You stay silent. Let the product speak.

**Second 55-60:** "That answers every call, 24/7, books the appointment, texts you the info. Fifty bucks a month, no contract."

**You talk 25 seconds. They talk 35 seconds. That ratio is right.**

## WALK-IN RULES (20)

1. Walk in with purpose. No pause at the door.
2. Clean polo, jeans, clean shoes. Not corporate, not sloppy.
3. Eye contact + nod before smiling. Let them speak first.
4. Open: "I'm Boston, I live here in Tyler. 60 seconds."
5. Never open with company name, title, or product description.
6. "I live here in Tyler" = your single strongest credibility line.
7. "I called your shop yesterday" = shifts from pitch to audit.
8. Always trigger the demo call. Words describe. Demo proves.
9. The 903 area code signals local. It matters.
10. Price comes AFTER the demo, never before.
11. Supply house: approach the youngest contractor, not the group.
12. Introduce yourself to the counter guy separately. Leave a card.
13. If the alpha raises an eyebrow, the group has permission.
14. Never challenge "let me think about it." Validate, leave a card.
15. The wife decides at the kitchen table. Give him ammo to show her.
16. Follow-up text 48 hours later during lunch. Use their name.
17. After 3 rejections, walk between shops. Don't sit in the car.
18. Set a daily CONTACT goal (8 owners), not a sales goal.
19. The age flip happens when they discover the competence — not when you claim it.
20. Honesty about limitations is the strongest trust signal.
