4. How the Sales Machine Works

BOSS Systems runs an automated lead-to-close pipeline. Here is every piece and what it does.

Step 1: Lead Discovery

The Lead Engine runs automatically. It pulls signals from weather alerts, Google reviews, news, and Reddit. When a heatwave hits Mississippi, it finds every HVAC company in the affected area. When someone on Reddit complains about a plumber not answering, it finds nearby plumbing companies. It then searches Google Places for those businesses, scores them on how likely they are to buy, and loads them into the call queue.

Right now the engine has 200+ leads scored and ready. It costs about $0.07 per lead. The Google Places budget is $300 (expires August 7, 2026) with $288 remaining.

Step 2: AI Cold Calls

The Auto Caller dials 50 leads per day, Monday through Friday. It runs at three times:

  • 10:00 AM CT (17 calls)
  • 12:30 PM CT (17 calls)
  • 3:00 PM CT (16 calls)

The AI opens by disclosing it is AI (FTC requirement), then asks about their missed calls. It does NOT try to close the sale. It qualifies and routes.

If the prospect is interested, it sends them to one of two paths:

  1. Self-service: Text them the analysis link (bosssystems.co/analyze.html) and let them explore on their own
  2. Guided: Schedule a call with the team

Step 3: Hot Lead Alert

When the AI caller gets a meeting, callback, or close, you get an instant notification on your phone through ntfy. The alert includes the business name, phone number, tier, and the signals that made them a good target. You call them back immediately.

Step 4: Mystery Caller (Free Leads)

The Mystery Caller calls businesses pretending to be a customer ("Hey, my AC quit and it is getting hot in here. Can somebody come out today?"). It grades how they handle the call. The grade becomes a lead magnet: "We mystery shopped your business. Here is your grade." That gets their attention and opens the conversation. This runs automatically through n8n.

Step 5: Email Sequence

After the initial contact, leads enter a 3-step email sequence. Step 1 is the mystery shop results. Step 2 is competitor comparison. Step 3 is a direct offer. The sequence runs automatically through a LaunchAgent on the Mac.

Step 6: Follow-Up and Close

Hot leads get personal follow-up. The Hot Lead Nurture workflow in n8n tracks warm prospects. You call them back, demo the product (have them call 903-522-4459), and close using the scripts below.

Step 7: Onboarding

Once they pay (Venmo @BosRoss), send them the onboarding form (bosssystems.co/onboard.html). The form collects their business info, tone, hours, services, Q&A, and never-say rules. The build takes 72 hours max. Then they go live.

6. The Sales Script

The Opening Question

Every conversation starts the same way. Doesn't matter if you're on the phone, walking into their shop, or at a supply house. You ask:

"When you're out on a job and a call comes in, what happens to it?"

Then shut up. Let them talk. Whatever they say next tells you exactly where to go.

15-Second Pitch

Use this at supply houses, in passing, or when someone asks what you do:

"We built an AI that answers contractor phones 24/7 for $50 a month. You miss a call, your competitor gets the job. This stops that. Want to hear it? Call this number."

60-Second Walk-In Script

Use this when you walk into a business:

[0-5 sec] "Hey, are you the owner? I'm [name]. I live here in [city]. Got something I want to show you, about 60 seconds."

[12-20 sec] "I actually called your shop yesterday around [time]. [What happened: rang out, voicemail, put on hold.] If I'm a customer whose pipe just burst, I'm calling the next guy."

[20-25 sec] PAUSE. Say nothing. Let the pain land.

[30-35 sec] Pull out your phone. "Call this number and hear what your customers would hear instead." Hand them the phone with (903) 522-4459 ready to dial.

[55-60 sec] "That answers every call, 24/7, books the appointment, texts you the info. Fifty bucks a month, no contract."

3-Minute Phone Pitch

"How many calls go to voicemail when you're on a job?"

[Wait for answer]

"Industry data says 62% of contractor calls go unanswered. And 85% of those people never call back. They call the next guy. At your average ticket, that's [$X] a year walking out the door."

"What we built handles that. AI answers every call to your business, 24/7. Sounds like a real person. Books the appointment, answers questions about your services and hours, and texts you every detail."

"Ruby charges $250 to $720 a month for human answering, plus overages. Smith.ai starts at $95 for the cheap plan. We do it for $50 a month flat. No overages, no contracts, cancel anytime."

"Want to hear what it sounds like? Call (903) 522-4459 right now. Talk to it like a customer. Takes 30 seconds."

[After demo call]

"We can usually have you live in a day or two. Want to get started this week?"

Paint the Picture (By Trade)

Use the scenario that matches their trade. Make them feel it.

HVAC: "July. 102 degrees. You're on a roof. Phone rings. Voicemail. That's a $1,500 AC replacement that just went to your competitor."

Plumber: "9 PM. Pipe burst. They call you. Voicemail. They call the next guy. $800 gone."

Electrician: "Hands in a panel. Phone buzzes. You check it an hour later. $450 job, gone."

Roofer: "Storm Saturday night. 15 calls Sunday morning. You're asleep. First roofer to answer books all 15."

Dentist: "Mid-procedure. New patient calls. Voicemail. They Google the next dentist and book in 30 seconds."

Law Firm: "In a deposition. Potential $50,000 case calls. Voicemail. They call the next attorney on the list."

Auto Repair: "Under a car. Hands covered in oil. Phone rings in the office. Nobody there to grab it. $600 brake job, gone."

Pest Control: "Termite season. You are in a crawl space. Three calls come in back to back. You get to them 2 hours later. Two already booked someone else."

The Close

Never ask "would you like to try this?" Ask "when":

"We can have you live by Wednesday or Friday. Which works better?"

If they hesitate, reframe the status quo:

"Right now, every call that hits voicemail costs you money. You're paying $7,333 a month to NOT answer your phone. The question is not whether to fix it. It is when."

7. Every Objection and How to Kill It

"Too expensive"

"What's one job worth to you? One caught call pays for 7 months of this. You're losing $5,000 a month minimum in missed calls. This costs $50."

"I don't need it"

"62% of contractor calls go unanswered. 85% never call back. Do you know where you stand? We can mystery shop your phone for free and show you."

"I already have someone answering"

"What happens when she's not available? Evenings, weekends, when she's at lunch? This is her backup so she's not chained to the phone 24/7."

Never say anything negative about the wife or office manager who answers. She is usually the decision maker.

"I need to think about it"

"Absolutely. What specifically, the cost or whether it works? Because if it is the cost, $50 is less than one missed call. And if it is whether it works, call the demo right now and find out."

Never accept a stall without naming the real blocker. "Think about it" always means something specific.

"I'm too busy right now"

"The fact that you're too busy is exactly why calls are going to voicemail. Can I call you Thursday at 5:30 when you're done for the day?"

"I don't trust AI"

"Don't trust my words either. Call this number. 30 seconds. If it sounds like a robot, I'll leave and never bother you again."

Never argue about AI. Let the demo do the work. Once they hear it, this objection dies.

"My customers want a real person"

"What's worse for a customer: a natural-sounding AI that answers in 2 seconds, or your competitor's human who answered first because your phone was ringing out?"

"Send me some info"

"I'd rather show you. Call this number. 20 seconds. If you're not impressed, I'll email you everything and stop bugging you."

If they still insist on info: send them bosssystems.co/faq.html and bosssystems.co/hear-it.html

"Call me back later"

"Is this something you're actually interested in, or are you being polite? Because if it is real interest, let's lock in a day and time so I don't have to keep calling."

"I answer all my calls"

"What about 9 PM on a Saturday when someone has a burst pipe? When you're under a house with both hands full? This catches the ones you can't."

"I got burned by something like this before"

"Don't trust my words then. 30 days. See the call logs yourself. Cancel if it doesn't do exactly what I said it would. No contract, no penalty."

"My wife handles the phones"

"That's great. This isn't replacing her. It is her backup so she's not chained to the phone 24/7. Can I talk to her? She should know about this."

This is a BUYING SIGNAL, not an objection. The spouse is the one in pain. Get her on the call.

"It's the slow season"

"Slow season means 3 calls a day instead of 10. Missing one of those three is missing 33% of your business for the day. Every call matters more when they're rare."

"I got a robocall from you guys"

"I apologize for that. I am removing you from our list right now. You will not hear from us again. Is there anything else I can help you with before I go?"

Do not argue. Do not pitch. Remove them immediately. If they are angry, let them vent, say "You're right, and I'm sorry," and move on. Never escalate.

"Are you recording my customers' calls?" / "What about data privacy?"

"All calls are processed by the AI in real time. We do not sell or share any data with anyone. You can see every call log and recording on your private dashboard. Only you have access. Our full privacy policy is at bosssystems.co/privacy.html."

"What if I just want to keep using my own phone?"

"You keep using your phone exactly the same way. This is a backup. When you can't answer, the AI picks up instead of voicemail. When you can answer, you answer. Nothing changes except you stop losing the calls you miss."

8. Text Message Playbook

Texts close more deals than phone calls with contractors. They read texts at red lights, at lunch, on the toilet. Keep every text under 3 sentences.

After a Good Call

"Hey [Name], good talking to you. Here's the demo number if you want to hear it again: (903) 522-4459. Call it anytime. No pressure either way."

After They Pay

"Got it. Thank you. I'm starting your build right now. You'll have a test call to listen to within 2 hours. Here's my number if you need anything: [YOUR PHONE NUMBER]"

Use your actual phone number here, not the demo line. The client needs to reach a human, not the AI.

System Goes Live

"Hey [Name], you're live. Your AI is answering [phone number] right now. Try calling it and talk to it like a customer. You'll get a text every time it takes a message or books something. If anything sounds off, text me and I'll fix it same day."

To the Spouse (Send Same Day as Setup)

"Hi [Spouse name], this is [your name] from BOSS Systems. I just set up the AI for [business name]. It answers all the calls [Owner] can't get to. Nights, weekends, while he's on a job. You'll never get a surprise bill. It is $50 a month flat, cancel anytime by texting me."

This is the most important text in all of onboarding. The spouse reviews the bank statement. If she doesn't know what this charge is, she kills it.

First After-Hours Catch

"Hey [Name], your AI just caught a call at [time]. After hours, would have gone to voicemail. The caller needed [service]. That's what $50 a month buys."

Before Month 1 Charge

"Hey [Name], month 1 update: your AI handled [X] calls, [Y] after hours, estimated [Z] in booked revenue. The $50 charge hits tomorrow. That's it, no surprises."

After a "No" (Send Once, Never Follow Up)

"No worries. If you ever want to hear what the AI sounds like, call (903) 522-4459 anytime. It's live 24/7. Have a good one."

Cancellation Save

"Would it help if we paused for a month instead? Everything stays saved. Your AI, your settings, your number routing. Zero charge. When you want it back on, just text me and it's 5 minutes."
Text Rules
1. Never send a text that reads like marketing. No exclamation points, no emojis.
2. Always use their name.
3. Specific beats generic: "$340 from 6 jobs" not "great earnings!"
4. One text per purpose. Never stack two asks in one message.
5. "No pressure either way" gets more replies than "Let me know ASAP."
