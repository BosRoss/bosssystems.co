The Sales Process (Start to Close)

Step 1: Check Their Profile

Before you call or visit, look at their card in the LEADS tab. It tells you whether they have a website, their Google rating, reviews, and what the recommended foot-in-the-door is. Go in knowing what to pitch.

Step 2: Open With Their Problem

If no website: "I was looking for [service] in [city] and I noticed your business does not have a website. You are showing up on Google Maps, but anyone searching on their phone is scrolling past you to the businesses with websites and reviews."

If has website: "I ran a quick analysis on your business. Found a couple of gaps that might be costing you jobs. Can I show you?" Then send them the Analyze tool.

If in person: "I called your shop yesterday. [What happened]. If I am a customer with a burst pipe, I am calling the next number."

Step 3: Show the Fix

For websites: Pull up a sample site on your phone. "This is what we build. Clean, fast, shows up on Google. Takes us 72 hours. $299 and it is done."

For phone/automations: Run the Analyze tool together. Walk through each gap the tool finds. "See this? This is money walking out the door. We can fix it."

Step 4: Close

"I have two build slots this week. Wednesday or Friday. Which works better?"

Do not ask "Do you want to sign up?" Give them two options that both mean yes.

Step 5: Expand

After the first thing is working, come back with: "Now that your [website/phone/reviews] is handled, let me show you what else we can automate." Send them bosssystems.co/services.html. The first win earns trust for everything else.

How to Use the Website Builder (TOOLS Tab)

Building a Website for a Client

Go to the TOOLS tab. Scroll to Website Builder. Enter the business name and city exactly as it appears on Google Maps.

Style Notes: Ask the client what they like. Do NOT paste a competitor URL — the result will look like a copy. Instead, write what they want in plain English: "clean layout, big hero photo, blue and white colors, no clutter, prominent phone number." The more specific, the better the website turns out.

Colors: Ask "What colors are on your truck/logo/uniform?" Match those. If they do not care, leave it blank and Lovable will pick professional colors for their trade.

What happens: The tool pulls their real Google data (rating, reviews, hours, phone, address). If the business already has a website, it automatically scrapes their existing site for images, text content, logo, and brand colors — so the new site keeps their real photos and info. If there is no existing website, it skips that step and uses Google data only. Either way, it generates a complete prompt, copies to your clipboard, and opens Lovable. Paste the prompt. Lovable builds the site in 2-3 minutes.

Existing Website vs No Website: If they already have a site, the builder pulls their actual images and content so Lovable builds an UPGRADE, not a blank slate. You will see "Pulled X images + content from existing site" in the status. If they have no website, the builder uses placeholder image slots instead and the client uploads photos later using the photo upload link (see below). Never use stock photos in either case.

Client Photo Upload System

After building the website, go to Client Image Upload Link in the TOOLS tab. Type the client name and click GENERATE UPLOAD LINK.

Send the link to the client. They see a simple page where they:
1. Pick which image to change (Hero Banner, Team Photo, Gallery 1-4)
2. Upload a photo from their phone or computer
3. Hit submit

You get notified. We swap the image on their site within 24 hours.

They can use this link anytime in the future — new project photo, new team member, seasonal update. Same link, same process. This is a selling point: "You can update your photos anytime from your phone. No calling us, no waiting."

Website-Specific Objections
"I don't need a website"
When someone in your town searches "[your trade] near me" on their phone, are you showing up? Without a website, you are invisible to anyone who is not already your customer. Your competitors with websites are getting those calls instead.
"I get all my work from word of mouth"
That is great and it means people trust you. But when someone gets a referral, the first thing they do is Google you. If they find nothing, some of them call the next name instead. A website makes sure your referrals actually convert.
"I already have a Facebook page"
Facebook is fine for people who already know you. But Google is where strangers find you — and Google ranks websites, not Facebook pages. A website with your services and number shows up in search results. A Facebook page usually does not.
Integration & Technology
"How does this work with my ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro?"
We connect to whatever you already use. ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, Google Calendar — we read from it and write to it. You do not switch systems. You do not learn anything new. Everything goes where it already goes.
"Do I need to change my phone number?"
No. We forward your existing number. Same number your customers already have. When you can not answer, it forwards to the AI. When you can answer, you answer like normal. Nothing changes for your customers.
"I'm not tech-savvy"
You do not need to be. Most of these systems run through text messages — you already know how to text. Setup is a 15-minute phone call where you tell us about your business. We build everything. You approve it. That is it.
"What if the AI says something wrong?"
The AI only says what you tell it to say. During setup, you give us your pricing ranges, your hours, your services. It never makes up information. If someone asks something it does not know, it says 'let me have the owner call you back' and texts you.
"My guys don't use apps"
Neither do ours. Everything runs through text messages. Your tech texts 'OTW 123' and the customer gets 'Your technician is on the way.' If they can text, they can use it.
"What happens if your system goes down?"
Calls go back to your phone, same as before. The AI is a safety net, not a replacement. If it is ever offline — which is rare — nothing breaks. Your phone just rings normally.
"Who sees my customer data?"
Only you. We do not sell data, share it, or use it for anything other than running your systems. Your customer list stays yours. You can export it anytime.
