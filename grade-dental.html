Questions from Dental Practice Owners

What scenario does the mystery shopper use?
The AI calls your office as a new patient with a dental problem. It asks about scheduling, availability, insurance, and next steps the same way a real patient would. Could be a toothache, a cosmetic question, or a new-patient inquiry. Your front desk won't know it's a test.
My front desk is always slammed. They're checking patients in and out while answering phones. Is this fair?
That's exactly why this matters. If your front desk is too busy to answer the phone properly, your new patients are experiencing the same thing. 85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back. Better to find out for $29 than lose a $10,000+ lifetime patient to the practice down the street that picks up on the first ring.
How fast do I get results?
Within 1 hour of payment. The call happens within minutes. Grading and report generation take a few more minutes. Your full report lands in your inbox typically within 30 minutes.
Will my front desk staff know it's a mystery shop?
No. The AI sounds like a real patient asking real questions about scheduling, insurance, and their dental concern. Your team won't know they're being evaluated, so you get an honest picture of what your patients actually experience when they call.
What if nobody answers?
That's the most important data you can get. If your office phone goes to voicemail during business hours, you'll get an F for Answer Speed and a clear picture of how many new patients you're likely losing. 62% of service calls go unanswered. 85% of those callers never call back. Now you'll know if you're one of them.
We use an answering service. Can I test them?
Yes. That's one of the best uses for Phone Grade. We call the number your patients call. We'll grade however that call is handled, whether it's your front desk, an answering service, or voicemail. Many dental offices discover their answering service is putting patients on hold, giving wrong office hours, or failing to book appointments.
Should I test during peak hours or slow hours?
Both tell you different things. Peak hours (Monday mornings, lunch hours) show how your front desk handles the rush when new patients are most likely calling. Slow hours show your baseline. Many practice owners do a monthly shop to track improvement and catch problems before they cost real patients.
